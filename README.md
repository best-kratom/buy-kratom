## Types of Kratom Products You Can Buy Online
The market has come a long way from just bitter kratom powders. Today, you can find kratom in a variety of formats that fit into a busy lifestyle or a relaxing evening ritual, depending on your preferred taste and onset duration.

### Kratom Powder
Powders are essentially dried kratom leaves ground into a fine dust. It’s the most cost-effective and versatile option. You can brew it into tea or use the "toss and wash" method. The taste is acquired, like earthy, bitter matcha.

### Kratom Capsules
If you’re fine with flavorless options, capsules are ideal. They contain the same powder but are tucked into a veggie or gelatin shell. They take a bit longer to kick in than powders since your body has to break down the capsule first. However, they’re perfect for discreetly taking your dose while out and about.

### Kratom Extracts
These are highly concentrated forms of kratom, containing most of the plant’s active alkaloids. You’ll usually see them labeled as 10x, 50x, or even "Full Spectrum." They’re much stronger than regular powder, so they’re better left to the seasoned pros who know their tolerance levels.

### Kratom Gummies
Gummies make taking kratom feel like grabbing a snack. They’re infused with kratom extract and flavored with fruit extracts to hide the herbal taste. They are incredibly convenient for consistent dosing, though they can be a bit pricier per serving than bulk powder.

### Kratom Tablets
Tablets are essentially pressed kratom powder or extract. They offer the convenience of capsules without the bulk of the shell. They’re easy to swallow and great for those who want a measured dose without the mess of measuring spoons.

### Liquid Kratom Shots
Commonly found in small 2-oz bottles, these are liquid extracts meant for quick consumption. They hit the system fast and are very potent. If you're looking for an "on-the-go" boost, these are the heavy hitters of the online kratom shop.

## Kratom Safety and Possible Side Effects

While millions of Americans use kratom daily, it’s not a "more is better" kind of supplement. If you overdo it, your body will have some unwanted side effects, like nausea, dry mouth, and constipation. An even bigger risk is developing a tolerance. 

You take too much, too frequently, without any breaks, and on some random day, the same dosage will not work for you. Kratom must be used responsibly. Keep your doses low, stay hydrated, and take regular breaks. Don't treat it like a limitless pill.

Look for a QR code or a link to lab results on the packaging. Verify the absence of pathogens (E. coli, mold), pesticides, heavy metals, and other impurities in kratom.
